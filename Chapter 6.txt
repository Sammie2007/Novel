The ruins were cold, but for the first time in his life, Jinwah felt a strange sense of safety. There were no white-robed Disciples here, and no terrifying Empress. There was only the sound of the wind whistling through the hollow stone of the old cathedral.
As he explored the collapsed archway, Jinwah’s foot caught on a metal ring in the floor. He cleared away the dirt and moss to find a heavy, reinforced hatch. It wasn't made of divine gold or Void-bone; it was made of thick, human steel.
He pulled it open.
Jinwah went down the stairs into a small room. A screen on the wall turned on. It started to explain the history.
A long time ago, the world was a single, peaceful place. Humans and Gods lived together in the same cities. The Gods were not called "The Lights" or "The Voidans" back then. They were just powerful beings who used their abilities to help humans. Humans were very smart and used advanced technology. Together, the Gods and the Humans built a great civilization where everyone was equal.
The Void did not belong in this world. It was a dark energy that came from deep space. It was not a person or a god; it was a type of cosmic energy that acted like a virus. When this energy reached the planet, it started to infect the Gods. Because the Gods were so powerful, the dark energy attached to their power and changed their bodies and their minds.
DÈÆ.TH was the first and most powerful God to be infected by this dark energy. He did not choose to be changed, but once the Void touched him, he became the first "Voidan."
The Gods who were not infected yet became very scared. They were afraid that if they stayed near the infected Gods or the humans, they would get sick too. To protect themselves, these "pure" Gods decided to split the world into pieces.
They moved high up into the sky and built the High Cities. They started calling themselves "The Lights" and wore white clothes to show they were clean and pure. They forced the humans to live in "Shelters" on the ground. They told the humans this was for their protection, but really, they wanted the humans to act as a shield between the High Cities and the infected Voidans living in the dark.
This was the start of the war. The Lights wanted to destroy the Voidans to stop the infection, and the Voidans wanted to get revenge for being kicked out of society.
The human records also mention a legend. The legend says that one day, a "Hybrid" would be born. This child would be half-human and half-Voidan. Usually, a human dies instantly if they touch Void energy. A Voidan is powerful but loses their human kindness. The legend says the Hybrid is the only being who can have the full power of the Void while keeping a human heart and mind. The machine says this person is a "bridge" between the two worlds.
However, the recording stopped there. The specific details of what the Hybrid is supposed to do to end the war were deleted or lost.
The screen flickered and died, leaving the room in a heavy, dusty silence.
Jinwah sat on the cold floor, his heart hammering against his ribs. The simple words of the machine had stripped away the lies of both factions. The Lights didn't want to save him; they wanted to study his immunity to the infection. The Empress didn't want to help him; she wanted to use his "clean" connection to the Void to finish DÈÆ.TH’s old war.
He looked at his hands. In the dark of the vault, they didn't look like weapons. They looked like the hands of a technician—someone meant to put things back together.
"The bridge," he whispered. The word felt heavy, a responsibility he hadn't asked for but finally understood.
He stood up, the exhaustion of his flight replaced by a cold, sharp clarity. The "sweetness" he had tried to protect wasn't just a personality trait; it was the anchor that kept the Void from consuming his soul. It was the only reason he wasn't a monster like Reo Deathstroke.
He climbed back up the stairs, stepping out from the hatch and into the grey light of the ruins. He looked up at the distant, shimmering glow of the High Cities, then down toward the direction of the Ossuary.
"Neither of you knows the ending," Jinwah said to the empty air, his voice low and firm. "You're both fighting for a world that's already dead."
He tightened his grip on the strap of his bag. He was a fugitive from the gods, a defector from the dark, and a mythic anomaly. But he wasn't lost anymore.
"I'm going to find the rest of the legend," he vowed. The wind picked up, tugging at his hood, but he didn't flinch. "I'm going to find the truth about what I am. And then I’m going to decide how this story ends."
For the first time since leaving Shelter-7, Jinwah Voidheart didn't feel like a victim. He felt like a protagonist.
End of Chapter Six
