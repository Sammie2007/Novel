While the Voidan Generals plotted in their dark halls, the High City of Caelum remained a place of eternal, golden peace. There were no shadows here, only white marble and the scent of fresh lilies. This was the home of the Lights—the gods who chose to stay "pure" and separated themselves from the world.
At the highest point of the city sat the Palace of Noon. Inside, seated on a throne of woven sunbeams, was Lightspeed. She was the Holy Mother of Light. She was not a queen; she was a sacred figure of peace. She was a vision of blinding beauty, with golden hair that reached the floor and eyes that glowed like stars. She spoke with a voice that was soft and kind, like a mother talking to a child, but her word was the absolute law.
Standing before her were the Disciples of RESURRECTION. They were the holy warriors of the Light. They were famous for being "angelic"—they were polite, beautiful, and they truly believed they were the heroes sent to save the world.
Glint stood at the front. He was the "Charisma Leader." He possessed a power called "Shiny Charisma." When he spoke, people felt a warm, happy feeling in their chests that made them want to follow him blindly. He was the perfect holy hero, leading the group with a smile that made everyone feel safe.
Beside him was Aurora Starblade, the "Celestial Warrior." She was their greatest fighter, a regal woman with a sword made of starlight. She had been the one sent to the market in Chapter 1, but she had failed to bring Jinwah back.
Nova Spire was next. Her power was the "Manipulation of Metals and Air." She could bend steel with her mind and control the wind. She was quiet and serious, responsible for building the floating palaces and the golden rooms where "unpure" beings were kept.
Windrunner Aetheria was the "Agile Runner." She was a scout who moved so fast she looked like a streak of white light. She was the one who could find anyone, anywhere, in the blink of an eye.
Helios Firestorm was the "Pyromancer." He controlled holy fire. He didn't see fire as a weapon for hurting people, but as a way of "washing" them. To him, burning away the Void was a beautiful act of love.
Finally, there was Quickblade. He was the "Lightning Fast Swordsman." He was a master of speed, able to strike a hundred times before a normal human could even draw a breath.
"He has escaped us again," the Holy Mother of Light said. Her voice was soft, but it sounded like a sad sigh that filled the whole palace. "Aurora, you were so close at the market. How did a simple technician slip through your fingers?"
Aurora Starblade knelt low, her head bowed in shame. "Holy Mother, his power was... unexpected. He did not just fight; he un-made the reality around him. He is more than a technician. The Void inside him is awake."
"Then we must go again," Glint said, his shiny charisma making the room feel hopeful and righteous despite the failure. "We cannot let him stay in the dark. If he stays with the Voidans, he will become a monster. If he stays in the ruins, he will die. We are the only ones who can save him."
"He is a sacred treasure," Aetheria added, her feet barely touching the ground. "He belongs here, where it is clean and safe. We will go back to the ruins and find him. This time, he will not escape."
"We must capture him with love," the Holy Mother of Light spoke, her radiant voice echoing through the hall like a warm summer breeze. "He is confused by his human heart. We must be gentle until we can show him that he is meant to be the father of a new, perfect race. He belongs in a golden garden, away from the dirt and the dark."
"I will prepare the air-lock sanctuary," Nova Spire said. "I will use the pressure of the wind to hold him gently this time so he cannot use his Void power to hurt himself or run away again."
"I will wrap him in my holy fire," Helios added with a kind smile. "It will make him feel warm and sleepy. He will wake up here, safe in our arms."
The Disciples truly believed they were the "good guys." They didn't want to hurt Jinwah; they wanted to "save" him by putting him in a beautiful, golden cage where they could control his power and his ability to have children. They were frustrated that he had gotten away the first time, and they were determined to succeed now.
"Go, my Disciples," the Holy Mother said, her radiant glow filling the palace. "Find Jinwah Voidheart. Bring him back to me. Tell him his struggle is over. Tell him the Light is coming to take him home."
The six Disciples turned and flew out of the palace, looking like six streaks of gold across the sky. They were heading for the ruins, and they were bringing a "peace" that was just as dangerous as any war.
End of Chapter Nine
