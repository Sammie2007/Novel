The Inner Circle of the Ossuary still smelled of burnt ozone and pulverized obsidian. The doors that Jinwah had disintegrated were gone, replaced by a shimmering, unstable veil of violet energy.
The Empress of Death stood in the center of the wreckage, her silhouette dark and motionless. Opposite her stood the heavy hitters of her army—the ones who hadn't been present to witness the "humane" technician turn into a cosmic supernova.
Reo Deathstroke stood at the front, his hand resting on the hilt of a jagged Void-blade. His skin, hardened like volcanic rock from his time in Ion, seemed to absorb the dim light of the room. Beside him, Gorthok the Unyielding crossed his massive arms, his breathing sounding like a slow-motion rockslide.
"You let him walk out," Reo said, his voice a low, raspy challenge. "A boy. A half-breed who spent his life fixing toasters in a human rat-hole."
"He did not 'walk' out, Reo," the Empress replied, her voice cold enough to frost the air. "He tore the reality of this chamber apart."
Reo spat on the bone-mosaic floor. "Because we weren't here. If I’d been at that gate, I would have snapped his neck before he could blink. You’re obsessed with this 'Hybrid' legend, Empress. It’s making you soft. He's just a fluke. A biological error."
Gorthok nodded, his voice deep and gravelly. "The boy is a coward. He ran to the ruins like a wounded dog. We do not need a 'bridge.' We have the Void. We have the strength."
The atmosphere in the room turned razor-sharp. Among the lieutenants, the sentiment was clear but unspoken: they thought the Empress was becoming a "funny woman"—an eccentric dreamer who was chasing a myth instead of finishing the war.
"The Lights are stagnant," Reo continued, pacing the wreckage. "They cannot reproduce. Their blood grows thin. In another century, they will be ghosts. And the humans? Their 'disgusting technology' is a joke. Steel and wires against the dark? We don't need a boy who can have children to win. We just need to keep killing until there’s nothing left to bury."
The Empress turned slowly, her eclipse-eyes burning with a dangerous, white-fire ring. The violet veil behind her flared, reflecting her rising anger. The lieutenants went still, realizing they had pushed the goddess too far.
Just as the tension reached a breaking point, a soft, mocking whistle echoed from the shadows of the arched ceiling.
Vesper Blackwood dropped down, landing perfectly between Reo and the Empress. Her light-bending robes flickered, showing a glimpse of her emerald eyes and a playful, sharp-toothed grin.
"Wow," Vesper said, clapping her hands slowly. "The ego in this room is actually thicker than the Void strain. I’m surprised the floor hasn't collapsed under the weight of Reo’s self-esteem."
Reo glared at her. "Get out of the way, Vesper. This is a matter of strategy."
"Strategy?" Vesper laughed, leaning back against a jagged pillar as if she didn't have a care in the world. "You call 'just keep killing' a strategy? That’s not a plan, Reo, that’s a hobby. A boring one, actually."
She turned to the Empress, her charisma acting like a lightning rod, pulling the heavy, suffocating tension out of the air.
"Look, the kid got scared. Can you blame him? He went from fixing power converters to being told he's a cosmic messiah in twenty minutes. Most people would have fainted; he just blew up a door. I’d call that a successful first interview."
Vesper winked at Gorthok, who looked confused, and then stepped closer to the Empress, her tone turning slightly more serious but remaining catchy and light.
"He’s out there in the ruins, probably crying over a broken rock. He’s humane, remember? He’s not going to the Lights—he hates their hypocrisy. And he’s not coming back here yet because, frankly, Reo is kind of a jerk. But he’s still ours."
The Empress’s fire dimmed slightly. Vesper was the only one who could talk to her like this—the only one who could treat a goddess like a person.
"Go find him," the Empress commanded, her gaze sweeping over all of them. "Reo, Gorthok—take the hunters. But if you touch a hair on his head without my order, I will show you why DÈÆ.TH called me his friend."
Reo grunted, a sign of reluctant obedience, and stomped out of the room. Gorthok followed.
Once they were gone, Vesper sighed and looked at the Empress. "You know Reo’s going to try and 'test' him, right? He doesn't believe in the Third Path."
"I know," the Empress whispered, looking out toward the entrance to the Grey Zones. "But if Jinwah is truly the Bridge, he will have to survive the Void before he can save the world."
Vesper adjusted her cloak, her emerald eyes flashing. "Don't worry, Boss. I'll make sure the kid doesn't get his heart broken... or his head removed. I’ve always liked a good mystery."
With a shimmer of her robes, Vesper vanished into the dark, leaving the Empress alone in her ruined sanctuary.
End of Chapter Seven
