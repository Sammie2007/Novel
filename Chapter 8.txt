Reo Deathstroke didn’t follow Jinwah into the ruins. Not yet. Instead, he marched into the Iron Hall, a place where the air was thick with the smell of scorched metal and old blood. This was the true heart of the Void world. While the Empress stayed in her tower dreaming of prophecies, these were the people who actually commanded the legions.
In this room, the Empress was called a "fanny woman"—a crazy dreamer who had gone soft. Without Vesper Blackwood around to listen, the talk was even meaner. They saw Vesper as a "snitch" and a "bootlicker," a girl who only stayed important because she acted like the Empress’s personal assistant. Ever since DÈÆ.TH vanished, they believed Vesper had been sucking up to the Empress just to keep a seat at the table.
Reo stood at the head of the stone table. Standing right behind his shoulder was Gorthok the Unyielding, his loyal sideman. Gorthok was a terrifying behemoth, a mountain of grey muscle and scarred skin who never left Reo’s side. He didn't speak much; he was the hammer that Reo used to crush anyone who disagreed.
The rest of the council was a collection of lethal beauty and dark power.
Khaos Nightshade sat to the left, looking like a dark goddess. She was breathtakingly beautiful, with long, ink-black hair that seemed to have a life of its own, swirling around her porcelain-pale shoulders. She wore a dress made of living shadows that clung tightly to her curves, shifting and moving like smoke. Her power was gravity. With a wave of her hand, she could crush a human soldier into a tiny ball of meat.
Next to her was Arachne Darkweaver. She was the seductive one, leaning back with a graceful, silver-haired elegance that made her look like a queen. Her eyes were glowing purple, and her voice was a hypnotic purr. She wore light, flowing silks that showed off her slim waist and long legs. She didn't use brute force; she used illusions to trap men in their own minds until they begged for death.
Across from them was Lumina Frostbite, and she was the weird one of the group. Despite being a deadly ice warrior, she was surprisingly friendly and bubbly. She had short, spiky blue hair and a very athletic, toned body that looked like it was carved from marble. She gave a cheerful wave to Reo as he sat down. She could freeze a person’s blood with a touch, but she was the only one in the room who actually looked like she enjoyed life.
Finally, there was Xandros Shadowreaver. He was the Necromancer. Pale, thin, and spooky, he sat in the corner like a skeleton in fine robes. He was the reason the Voidans never lost. Every human or Light that fell in battle became a shadow-soldier for his army.
"The Empress has lost her mind," Reo growled, the volcanic heat of his skin making the air shimmer. "She thinks this Jinwah is special. A boy with an average technician brain. He spent twenty years fixing toasters! He’s a pesky human who got lucky because we weren't there to stop him."
"He looked pretty powerful in the footage," Lumina chirped, her voice light and musical. She created a small ice-sculpture of a flower on the table just for fun. "He blew up half a market! I think he’s kind of cute. Maybe we should be his friend?"
"We don't need friends," Khaos snapped, her porcelain face twisting into a sneer. "And we don't need a boy who can reproduce. Being 'humane' is a disease. We have Gorthok to break their bones and Xandros to raise their corpses. Why should we care about a hybrid?"
"Because the Empress is obsessed," Arachne said, her purple eyes spinning with dark light. "And Vesper is whispering in her ear. That snitch wants the boy around because he’s her only ticket to staying important. She bootlicks the Empress every day just to make sure we don't kick her out of the Ossuary."
"Vesper is nothing," Gorthok rumbled, his voice sounding like two boulders grinding together. It was the first time he had spoken, and the room went quiet. "The boy is scrap metal. We find him. We end him."
The argument went on for hours. They discussed the political power they held—how the humans in the shelters feared them, not the Empress’s prophecies. To them, the "Third Path" was a joke. They believed the Lights were dying because they couldn't have children, and that the Voidans would eventually win through raw, bloody strength.
"Let the fanny woman dream," Reo finally said, standing up. "We are the ones who fight. Gorthok and I will lead the hunt. If the technician is as 'average' as I think, I’ll let Gorthok sit on him. If he’s actually 'OP'... then I’ll show him what it’s like to fight a real god."
Lumina sighed, looking a little sad for Jinwah, but she stood up with the others. The council was decided. They were going to the ruins, and they weren't going there to talk. They were going there to prove that in a world of gods and monsters, a human heart was just a target.
End of Chapter Eight
