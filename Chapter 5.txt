"I don't want your paths."
Jinwah’s voice was small, but it cut through the heavy silence of the Empress’s chamber like a blade. He was shaking, his hands balled into fists so tight his knuckles were white.
The Empress narrowed her eclipse-eyes. "You speak as if you have a choice, little hybrid. The Light is hunting you. The Void is claiming you. There is no middle ground."
"I want to be human!" Jinwah suddenly shouted, the frustration of the last forty-eight hours finally boiling over. "I want to go back to my stall. I want to fix converters. I don't want to be a 'key' or a 'messiah' or a 'brood-stallion.' I just want to be nothing!"
The Empress’s face didn't just change; it transformed. The starlight-pale skin darkened, and the violet light in the room flared into a blinding, angry crimson.
"NOTHING?"
Her voice didn't just fill the room; it shook the foundations of the Ossuary. It was a sound of ancient, divine fury—the sound of a goddess who had spent eons waiting for a miracle, only to be told it wanted to be a technician.
"YOU ARE BORN OF THE STARS AND THE DARKNESS, AND YOU BEG FOR THE MUD? YOU WOULD REJECT THE LEGACY OF DÈÆ.TH FOR THE SAKE OF A BROKEN TOASTER?"
The ground beneath Jinwah’s feet began to crack. The Empress didn't move, but the sheer force of her anger felt like a physical hammer.
Jinwah didn't think. He didn't plan. He simply broke.
The "sweet and humane" young man vanished beneath a tidal wave of cosmic survival instinct. The Void within him responded to the Empress’s shout with an equal and opposite reaction.
He didn't shapeshift—he erupted.
The nebula skin didn't just flicker; it blazed. The black-hole eyes opened wide, and a shockwave of absolute un-making tore out from his chest. The obsidian doors of the Inner Circle didn't just open; they disintegrated into fine, black sand.
"Jinwah, stop!" Vesper’s voice screamed from somewhere in the hallway, but he couldn't hear her.
He ran.
He didn't know how he was doing it. His feet barely touched the bone-mosaic floors. Every time a guard or a Voidan tried to step in his way, his power lashed out automatically, a shimmering veil of dark energy that simply pushed reality aside. He wasn't attacking them; he was denying them.
He reached the Great Rib-Gate. The soldiers of the shadow raised their weapons, but Jinwah slammed his hands together in mid-air. The resulting boom was silent—a vacuum of sound that sucked the breath from the room and hurled the guards into the liquid shadow below.
He burst through the entrance fissure and out into the Grey Zones.
He didn't use the Shadow-Skiff. He ran. His legs moved with a speed that blurred the world into a smear of grey and violet. He was "OP" in the most terrifying way—a god-tier engine being driven by a panicked twenty-year-old who didn't know where the brakes were.
The Void strain flared, a crown of dark energy burning around his head, lighting up the industrial wasteland like a falling star.
I have to get away, his mind repeated in a frantic loop. Away from the Light. Away from the Empress. Just... away.
Hours passed. Or maybe minutes. Time meant nothing when your blood was made of starlight.
Eventually, the energy began to fade, leaving him as quickly as it had come. The nebula skin receded, the black energy lines vanished, and Jinwah collapsed face-first into the dirt.
He was back in his human form—thin, exhausted, and utterly lost.
The industrial spires of the High Cities were nowhere to be seen. The violet glow of the Ossuary was a memory. He was in a place where the air was thick with the smell of old stone and dead ivy.
He pushed himself up, his muscles screaming in protest. He was in the middle of a massive, silent clearing.
Surrounding him were the ruins of something ancient—something from before the shelters, before the "Split." It was a cathedral, its spires snapped like dry twigs, its stained glass replaced by curtains of thick, grey moss. Massive stone statues of forgotten kings lay toppled in the courtyard, their faces worn away by centuries of acidic rain.
It was a ruin of the old human world.
Jinwah crawled toward the shelter of a collapsed archway. He was alone, he was powerless, and for the first time in his life, he was truly in the middle of nowhere.
He looked at his hands, now just normal, human hands covered in dirt.
"I'm human," he whispered to the silence, as if trying to convince the ruins. "I'm still human."
But deep in his marrow, the Void was still humming, waiting for him to wake up.
